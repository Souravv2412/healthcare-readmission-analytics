A comprehensive overview of the dataset, analytical framework, preprocessing decisions, modelling approach, and known limitations of this healthcare analytics capstone project.

University Final Project - Healthcare Analytics

Predicting 30-Day Hospital Readmissions
in Diabetic Patients

Hospital readmissions within 30 days represent a major cost driver for healthcare systems and often signal gaps in post-discharge care. This project develops a binary risk-scoring tool that flags high-risk diabetic patients at the point of discharge, enabling clinical teams to intervene with follow-up calls, medication reviews, and structured care plans.

Statistical Hypothesis Framework
Test selection logic and validation approach
Test Selection Logic
• Continuous vs binary target: Shapiro-Wilk normality check on 5,000-sample subsets. All continuous features violated normality → Mann-Whitney U selected over t-test.
• Categorical vs binary target: Chi-Square test of independence with expected-count verification (0 cells < 5 in all tests).
• Effect size always reported: Large n (≈100k) can make trivial differences statistically significant. Cliff's delta and Cramér's V provide practical context.
Pre-Test Data Validity
• Impossible readmission exclusion: 2,423 rows with expired or hospice discharge codes excluded before testing. These patients cannot logically be readmitted within 30 days.
• Analysis sample: 99,343 rows (from 101,766) at 11.39% readmission rate.
• Significance threshold: α = 0.05. All four hypotheses produced p < 0.0001.
